Description

Polyculture is the front for Joel McDonald, an independent game designer focused on making beautiful and interesting games.

History

Joel McDonald got his start way back in the year 2000 when he started dabbling in the Quake modding scene. After many years of active participation in the mod community, he became interested in the budding Quake eSports community. In 2006 he released the Quake 4 map, Placebo Effect, which became a fixture in the Quake 4 competitve scene, being selected for a number of events including the World Series of Video Games in 2006.

That same year, Joel started his job as level designer at Raven Software, his first title being the Wolfenstein reboot. He would then go on to contribute to a number of AAA titles, including Singularity and Call of Duty. In addition to working as level designer, Joel served as game programmer, scripter, and system designer during his years at Raven. Most importantly, during this time Joel discovered that video games are actually pretty cool.

In late 2013, Joel decided to throw away his promising career as AAA level designer on one of the largest game franchises in history and strike out on his own. After taking some time to decompress and prototype, Joel stumbled onto his first project, Prune, in May 2014.
